# Vita Press Church Launch — Implementation Plan

> **For Claude:** REQUIRED SUB-SKILL: Use superpowers:executing-plans to implement this plan task-by-task.

**Goal:** Launch Vita Press as a fresh-pressed juice business at our local church, generating real revenue while teaching our 13-year-old daughter entrepreneurship.

**Architecture:** Four-phase rollout — Pre-Launch (regulatory + supplies + recipe testing), Soft Launch (free samples + first sales), Full Launch (data-driven production + pre-orders), Month 2+ (Instagram marketing + expansion). Each phase has a go/no-go gate before proceeding.

**Key Constraint:** £200-500 budget. Robot Coupe 80 already owned. Church community of 100-300, predominantly Black African descent.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Week 0 — target: 2 weeks to complete)

### Task 1: Food Business Registration

**Action:** Register as a food business with your local council's Environmental Health team.

**Steps:**
1. Go to your local council website → search "register a food business"
2. Complete the online form (free). You'll need: business name (Vita Press), home address (production site), type of food (fresh juice, non-alcoholic beverages), start date
3. Submit — you must register **at least 28 days before** your first sale

**Done when:** Confirmation email/reference number received from council.

**Note:** You do NOT need to wait for an inspection before selling. Registration is a notification, not an approval. An inspection may follow later.

---

### Task 2: Level 2 Food Hygiene Certification

**Action:** Complete Level 2 Food Hygiene certificate online. Your daughter can do this alongside you — it's excellent learning.

**Steps:**
1. Choose a provider (e.g., highspeedtraining.co.uk or foodhygienecompany.co.uk — ~£25)
2. Complete the course (2-3 hours, can be done in one sitting)
3. Download and save the certificate

**Done when:** Certificate downloaded and saved.

---

### Task 3: Get Church Permission

**Action:** Speak to your pastor or church administrator to get permission and agree logistics.

**Steps:**
1. Request a brief meeting or conversation after service
2. Explain: your daughter is starting a small juice business as an entrepreneurship project, you'd like to sell fresh juice after Sunday services
3. Agree on: setup location (near the existing food/drink area), whether you need a table or bring your own, any contribution to the church (e.g., tithe on profits, free juice for pastors)
4. Ask if they can announce it the Sunday before your soft launch

**Done when:** Verbal or written confirmation from church leadership. Setup location agreed.

---

### Task 4: Order Bottles, Caps & Labels

**Action:** Source packaging supplies.

**Steps:**
1. **Bottles + caps:** Order 330ml with tamper-evident caps
   - 50x 330ml bottles
   - Suppliers: [Ampulla](https://www.ampulla.co.uk) or [BottleBox](https://www.bottlebox.co.uk)
   - Budget: ~£35 for 50
2. **Labels:** Design and order 50 self-adhesive labels
   - Use Vistaprint or Avery (design online)
   - Template: white/cream background, Vita Press logo, blend name in colour accent, ingredients list, "Made fresh in London", "Keep refrigerated — drink within 5 days", QR code to Instagram
   - 4 variants: 15x Skin Boost, 15x Immunity Boost, 10x Detox, 10x Liver & Kidney
   - Budget: ~£10
3. **Tasting cups:** Order 100x small sample cups (2oz/60ml) with lids — Amazon, ~£5

**Done when:** Orders placed. Expected delivery: 3-7 business days.

**Label design note:** Keep it simple for v1. Canva (free) works well. Must include all UK legal requirements:
- Business name and address
- Ingredients (descending order by weight)
- "No known allergens" (or list any if applicable)
- Net quantity: 330ml
- Best before: ______ (hand-write or stamp on each bottle)
- Storage: "Keep refrigerated, drink within 5 days"

### Task 6: Test Batch & Family Taste Test

**Action:** Produce 5 bottles of each blend (20 total) and taste test with family.

**Steps:**
1. Wash and prep all fruit/veg
2. Juice each blend separately in the Robot Coupe 80 — clean between blends
3. Pour into bottles, cap, label with blend name (handwritten is fine for test batch)
4. Refrigerate immediately
5. Family taste test: rate each blend 1-10 on taste, sweetness, appearance
6. Record results and any adjustments needed (more ginger? more lime? etc.)

**Done when:** All 4 blends tasted and scored. Adjustments noted. Recipes finalised.

**Daughter's role:** She helps with prep, operates the juicer (supervised), and leads the taste test scoring.

### Task 7: Create Sales Tracker

**Action:** Set up a simple tracking system for sales, costs, and inventory.

**Options (pick one):**
- **Notebook** (simplest): ruled notebook with columns for Date, Blend, Qty Made, Qty Sold, Revenue, Ingredient Cost, Profit
- **Google Sheet** (better for learning): same columns, auto-calculates totals and profit
- **Printed template**: design a weekly sheet, print copies

**Minimum columns:**
```
Date | Blend | Qty Made | Qty Sold | Price Each | Revenue | Ingredient Cost | Bottle Cost | Profit
```

**Done when:** Tracker ready to use from Week 1. Daughter understands how to fill it in.

## Phase 2: Soft Launch (Weeks 1-2)

### Task 10: Produce First Sales Batch

**Action:** Juice 50 bottles for your first Sunday (15x Skin Boost, 15x Immunity Boost, 10x Detox, 10x Liver & Kidney).

**Steps:**
1. Wednesday: Plan quantities — use the weighted split (15/15/10/10)
2. Thursday/Friday: Buy ingredients from New Covent Garden Market (10pm-6am)
3. Friday evening: Juice, bottle, and label all 50 bottles
4. Hand-write or stamp "Best before" date on each label (5 days from production)
5. Refrigerate overnight
6. Saturday: Pack into cool box with ice packs. Prepare tasting cups, cash float (£20 in coins), tablecloth

**Done when:** 50 labelled bottles refrigerated and ready for Sunday.

---

### Task 11: First Sunday — Samples & Sales

**Action:** Set up at church, give free samples, sell bottles.

**Steps:**
1. Arrive early, set up table near existing food/drink area
2. Display: tablecloth, 2-3 bottles of each blend visible, laminated or printed A4 with blend names and prices
3. Pour small tasting cups of each blend — let people try before buying
4. Pricing on display: **330ml £3.50 / 2 for £6.00 / All 4 flavours £12.00**
5. Your daughter handles sales and customer conversations — you support from behind
6. After every sale, note it down (blend, quantity, any feedback)
7. Collect WhatsApp numbers from interested buyers: "Want to pre-order next week? Give us your number!"

**Done when:** First Sunday complete. Sales counted, feedback collected, WhatsApp numbers gathered.

---

### Task 12: Week 1 Review

**Action:** Sunday evening — your daughter records Week 1 results.

**Steps:**
1. Count remaining stock per blend
2. Fill in sales tracker: what sold, what didn't, total revenue, total cost
3. Write down all customer feedback (favourite flavours, complaints, suggestions)
4. Calculate: which blend sold most? Which had leftovers?
5. Discuss: any recipe tweaks needed? Any operational issues (ran out of change, cool box too small, etc.)?

**Done when:** Sales tracker updated. Top sellers identified. Any issues noted with fixes for Week 2.

---

### Task 13: Week 2 — Adjust & Repeat

**Action:** Second Sunday, adjusted based on Week 1 data.

**Steps:**
1. Produce more of the top sellers, fewer of the slow movers
2. If any blend got zero or near-zero sales, consider dropping or reformulating
3. Repeat the Sunday setup with improvements from Week 1
4. Send first WhatsApp broadcast to collected numbers (Thursday): this week's menu + order by Friday
5. Any pre-orders: prepare those bottles with the customer's name on them

**Done when:** Week 2 complete. Two weeks of sales data in tracker.

---

### Phase 2 Gate: Continue or Pivot?

After 2 Sundays, confirm:
- [ ] At least 15+ bottles sold per Sunday (if less, investigate: pricing? flavours? visibility?)
- [ ] Top 2-3 flavours identified
- [ ] At least 5 WhatsApp numbers collected for pre-orders
- [ ] No food safety concerns raised
- [ ] Daughter is confident handling sales conversations

**If fewer than 10 bottles sold across both weeks:** Pause and reassess — the issue might be pricing, flavour selection, or visibility/placement at church.

## Phase 3: Full Launch (Weeks 3-6)

### Task 14: Data-Driven Production

**Action:** Shift from equal production to demand-led production.

**Steps:**
1. Review 2 weeks of sales data
2. Rank blends by sales volume
3. Week 3 production split example (42 bottles):
   - Top seller: 10 bottles
   - #2: 9 bottles
   - #3: 8 bottles
   - #4: 6 bottles
   - #5: 5 bottles
   - #6: 4 bottles
4. Your daughter decides the split — this is her inventory management lesson

**Done when:** Daughter is setting production quantities based on data, not guesswork.

---

### Task 15: Launch WhatsApp Pre-Orders Fully

**Action:** Run the full pre-order cycle weekly.

**Steps:**
1. Every Thursday: daughter sends the weekly menu via WhatsApp broadcast
2. Collect orders by Friday evening
3. Pre-orders are made and labelled with the customer's name
4. Remaining production is for walk-up sales at church
5. Track pre-order vs walk-up sales separately in the tracker

**Done when:** Pre-order cycle running weekly. Daughter managing the WhatsApp channel independently.

---

### Task 16: Create Price Menu & Display

**Action:** Make the church stall look more professional.

**Steps:**
1. Design a laminated A4 price menu (Canva):
   - Vita Press logo at top
   - All 4 blends with one-line descriptions
   - Prices: 330ml £3.50 / 2 for £6.00 / All 4 flavours £12.00
2. Print and laminate (library or Ryman's, ~£2)
3. Optional: small chalkboard or stand for the table

**Done when:** Professional price menu on display at church stall.

---

### Task 17: Introduce Bundles

**Action:** Start offering combo deals.

**Steps:**
1. Add to price menu:
   - 2 for £6.00 (saves £1.00)
   - All 4 flavours: 4x 330ml = £12.00 (saves £2.00)
2. Pre-make a few "all 4 flavours" bundles in a bag or grouped with a rubber band
3. Track bundle sales separately — this tells you what customers prefer

**Done when:** Bundles on offer, sales tracked.

---

### Phase 3 Gate: Sustainable?

After 6 weeks total, confirm:
- [ ] Averaging 25+ bottles per Sunday consistently
- [ ] Revenue covering ingredient and packaging costs (self-sustaining)
- [ ] Daughter managing WhatsApp orders and production planning with minimal help
- [ ] At least 10 regular/repeat customers
- [ ] Sales tracker maintained weekly

## Phase 4: Month 2+ Growth

### Task 18: Launch Instagram Content

**Action:** Your daughter starts posting to Instagram.

**Content ideas (1-2 posts per week):**
1. Behind-the-scenes juicing process (video/reel)
2. "What's in your bottle" ingredient flat-lay photos
3. Sunday setup at church
4. Customer reactions (ask permission first)
5. "Blend of the week" spotlight

**Done when:** First 3 posts published. Posting rhythm of 1-2x/week established.

---

### Task 19: Evaluate Product Line

**Action:** Use 6 weeks of data to make product decisions.

**Steps:**
1. Rank all 6 blends by total units sold
2. Bottom performer: consider dropping, replacing, or reformulating
3. If there's clear demand for something new (customer requests), test it
4. If approaching December: develop and test the Sorrel & Hibiscus seasonal blend

**Done when:** Product line decision made — keep, drop, or add based on data.

---

### Task 20: Explore Midweek Sales

**Action:** Test demand beyond Sunday church.

**Options to try (pick one):**
1. Offer midweek delivery to neighbours/friends (WhatsApp orders, deliver locally)
2. Take a batch to a weekday workplace or community event
3. Sell leftover Sunday stock via WhatsApp on Monday/Tuesday

**Done when:** One midweek channel tested. Decision made on whether to continue.

---

### Task 21: Month 2 Financial Review

**Action:** Your daughter does a full month-end review.

**Steps:**
1. Total up all revenue for the month
2. Total up all costs (ingredients, packaging, transport)
3. Calculate gross profit
4. Discuss: What should we do with the profit?
   - Reinvest (more stock, better packaging)?
   - Save toward can seaming equipment?
   - Pay herself a small amount?
5. Set a revenue target for Month 3

**Done when:** First monthly P&L complete. Reinvestment decision made. Month 3 target set.

**This is the single most important entrepreneurship lesson in the whole plan.** Make it a proper sit-down conversation.

---

## Graduation Trigger: When to Move to Cans

Do NOT invest in canning until ALL of these are true:
- [ ] 40+ bottles sold per week for 4 consecutive weeks
- [ ] Top 3 flavours clearly established
- [ ] Monthly profit consistently positive
- [ ] Daughter confident in full production cycle
- [ ] Budget available for can seamer (~£300-600) without risking cash flow

When ready, refer to the existing Vita Press business plan for the can transition strategy.
