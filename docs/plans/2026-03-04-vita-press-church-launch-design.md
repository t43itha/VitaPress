# Vita Press - Church Launch Design

**Date**: 2026-03-04
**Context**: Side hustle to teach entrepreneurship to 13-year-old daughter. Initial market is local church community (100-300 people, predominantly Black African descent, London area). Existing food/drink sales culture at church. Equipment: Robot Coupe 80 (already owned). Budget: £200-500.

**Approach**: Fresh-pressed juice in branded PET bottles sold at church, with WhatsApp pre-orders to reduce waste. Graduate to aluminium cans once top flavours are validated and demand is consistent.

## 2. Packaging & Branding

- **Bottles**: Clear PET with tamper-evident caps (Ampulla or BottleBox UK) — 250ml (~60p), 330ml (~65p), 500ml (~75p)
- **Labels**: Self-adhesive printed labels (Vistaprint/Avery), ~15p each, each blend gets own colour accent on consistent template
- **Total packaging cost per bottle**: 250ml ~75p, 330ml ~80p, 500ml ~90p

### Label requirements (UK law):
- Business name and address
- Full ingredients list (descending order by weight)
- Allergen info
- Net quantity (250ml / 330ml / 500ml)
- Best before date
- Storage conditions ("Keep refrigerated, drink within 5 days")

### Brand elements:
- Clean, minimal design - let juice colour contrast against white/cream label
- QR code linking to Instagram
- Tagline: "Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food"
- Consistent with existing Vita Press brand assets (orange accent, Instrument Serif + DM Sans)

## 4. Operations & Production

### Weekly cycle:
| Day | Activity | Who |
|-----|----------|-----|
| Wednesday | Check pre-orders (WhatsApp), plan quantities | Daughter |
| Thursday | Shop for ingredients | Together |
| Friday evening | Juice, bottle, label, refrigerate | Together |
| Saturday | Overflow production if needed, pack cool bag | Daughter |
| Sunday | Sell at church, collect feedback, count stock & cash | Daughter leads |
| Sunday evening | Record sales, costs, and notes in tracker | Daughter |

### Robot Coupe 80 production:
- 40-50 bottles (mixed 250ml/330ml/500ml) in ~60-90 minutes
- Juice in batches by flavour to avoid cross-contamination
- Wash and prep fruit before juicing

### Food safety:
- Register food business with local council (free, 28 days lead time)
- Level 2 Food Hygiene certification (£25, 2-3 hours online)
- Clean kitchen, separate from general use during production
- Label every bottle: "made on" and "best before" date (5 days)
- Keep batch log: date, ingredients, quantities, issues

### Cold chain:
- Refrigerate from production to sale
- Cool box with ice packs for Sunday transport
- Keep bottles in cool box at church, few on display at a time
- Unsold bottles: sell via WhatsApp midweek (3-4 days shelf life remaining)

### WhatsApp pre-orders:
- Broadcast list or small group for church customers
- Thursday: post week's menu, take orders by Friday evening
- Daughter manages the channel

## 5. Launch Plan

### Pre-launch (Week 0):
- Register food business with local council
- Complete Level 2 Food Hygiene cert
- Test batch at home - one of each flavour, family taste test
- Get pastor/church leadership permission, agree on setup location
- Daughter creates Vita Press Instagram page

### Soft launch (Weeks 1-2):
- Bring 30-40 bottles + tray of free tasting cups
- Free samples of all 6 flavours
- Sell at £3.50 (330ml) / £2.00 kid (250ml) / Parent+Kid combo £5.00
- Collect feedback in notebook
- Launch WhatsApp pre-order list

### Full launch (Week 3+):
- Production based on Week 1-2 data
- Laminated A4 price menu on display
- Active WhatsApp pre-orders
- Introduce family bundle and 500ml option

### Month 2 milestones:
- First Instagram content (behind-the-scenes, Sunday setup, customer reactions)
- Evaluate top 2-3 flavours
- Consider midweek secondary sales (neighbours, colleagues)
- If near December: launch Sorrel & Hibiscus limited edition
